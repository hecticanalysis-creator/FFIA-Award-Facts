Part-time written agreement — required inclusions
A written agreement is mandatory at the time of engagement. Any variation to the agreed pattern must also be recorded in writing — a text message counts, but it must be done by end of the affected shift at the latest.
#The agreement must include
1Number of ordinary hours to be worked each day
2Days of the week the employee will work
3Actual start and finish times each day
4When meal breaks may be taken and their duration
5That minimum daily engagement is 3 consecutive hours
6That any variation will be recorded in writing
Part-time hours outside the agreement = overtime. If a part-time employee works hours beyond their agreed pattern and there is no written record of a variation, those hours must be paid at overtime rates. Verbal agreements are not sufficient.
Right to disconnect — clause 13A
Effective from 26 August 2024 (26 August 2025 for small businesses). Employees have a right to disconnect from work-related contact outside their rostered hours. Employers must not directly or indirectly prevent an employee from exercising this right. Sending roster changes or work requests via text/messaging apps after hours may be caught by this provision.

Laundry allowance trigger. If your business supplies the uniform AND launders it (or pays for laundering), the allowance does not apply. It only triggers when the employee is required to wash their own uniform at home. Requiring a general dress code or a specific colour the employee could reasonably already own is not "special clothing" under the award.

Notice of resignation — by employee
The notice an employee is required to give is the same as the employer's obligation above (based on their length of service), except no additional notice is required based on age. If an employee aged 18+ fails to give the required notice, the employer may deduct up to one week's wages from their final pay — but only up to that one week maximum.
Job search entitlement during notice period
When an employer gives notice of termination or redundancy, the employee is entitled to up to one day of paid leave during the notice period to attend job interviews. The timing must be convenient to the employee, agreed with the employer. If requested, the employee must provide proof of attendance.
Summary dismissal
Summary dismissal (instant dismissal) is only available in cases of serious misconduct. No notice is required, but the employer must still pay out all accrued entitlements (annual leave, etc.). Always consult HR or an IR advisor before proceeding with summary dismissal — unfair dismissal claims are a significant risk if the process is not followed correctly.

Quick answers to the questions location managers actually get asked mid-shift. These are general guides — for complex situations always escalate to HR or your IR contact.
Pay queries
"Why is my Sunday pay different to Sarah's?"
Under the FFIA, Sunday rates differ by classification level. L1 employees receive 125% on Sunday, while L2 and L3 employees receive 150%. If Sarah is classified at a higher level, her Sunday rate will be higher. Check the classification on file for both employees.
"I only worked 2 hours — why am I being paid for 3?"
The FFIA requires a minimum 3-hour engagement for casuals and part-time employees per shift. Even if the actual shift was shorter, the minimum payment applies. This is a legal entitlement, not an error.
"I didn't get a break on my 6-hour shift — what happens?"
Shifts over 5 hours require a 30–60 minute unpaid meal break. If it wasn't provided, overtime rates apply from the point the break should have been taken until it was eventually given. This is a pay obligation, not just a conditions breach. Report it to your payroll team.
"Am I getting the right pay for working Christmas Day?"
Christmas Day is a public holiday. Permanent employees receive 225% of their base rate plus a substitute day off. Casuals receive 250%. If you're a permanent employee and weren't offered a substitute day, that also needs to be rectified.
"I've been casual for 8 months — can I go permanent?"
Under the Closing Loopholes No.2 Act 2024, eligible casuals can request conversion to permanent after 6 months of regular work (12 months for small businesses). The request must be made in writing. The employer must respond in writing within 21 days. Direct them to put the request in writing to the location manager or HR.
"Do I get paid extra for working past 10pm?"
Yes. Work between 10pm and midnight on weekdays attracts 110% of the base rate. Work between midnight and 6am is 115%. These evening penalties apply on top of any applicable weekend rates if the shift crosses into Saturday or Sunday.
Rostering & hours queries
"You've changed my hours without asking me."
For part-time employees with a written pattern of work agreement, any variation must be agreed in writing. A one-off change must be recorded by end of the affected shift. An ongoing change must be agreed in writing before it takes effect. If neither was done, the original hours stand and additional time worked is overtime.
"You can't contact me about work on my days off."
Employees have a right to disconnect from work-related contact outside their rostered hours under the Fair Work Act (clause 13A, effective August 2024). Employers cannot prevent employees from exercising this right. Reasonable contact for genuine emergencies may be different — but routine roster messaging on days off is caught by this provision.
"I worked 10 hours overtime last week — do I have to take time off?"
By default, overtime is paid in cash at the applicable overtime rate. An employer and employee can agree in writing to take time off instead (TOIL), but this requires mutual agreement. The employee cannot be forced to take TOIL. If agreed, TOIL must be taken within 6 months.
"I finished at midnight and I'm rostered at 8am — is that ok?"
No — the award requires a minimum 10-hour break after overtime. If an employee worked overtime and doesn't get 10 consecutive hours off before the next shift, double time (200%) applies from when they start until they've had the 10-hour break. Adjust the roster to ensure the gap is at least 10 hours.
Leave queries
"I always work Sundays — shouldn't my leave be at the Sunday rate?"
Annual leave loading is the greater of 17.5% or the weekend penalty rate the employee would have received on those days. If an employee regularly works Sundays at 150%, and they take leave over a Sunday, they should receive 150% — not just 17.5%. Check this is being applied correctly in your payroll system.
"A public holiday fell on my day off — do I get anything?"
Only if the public holiday falls on a day the employee is ordinarily rostered to work. If a full-time employee's regular day off happens to fall on a public holiday, they are not entitled to an extra day. Casuals are only paid if they actually work the public holiday.
Top compliance risks — FFIA MA000003
✕
Sunday rates vary by level — L1 ≠ L2/L3 Permanent L1 gets 125% on Sunday. Permanent L2 and L3 get 150%. Applying a flat Sunday rate across a mixed-level roster is a near-certain underpayment. This is unique to FFIA and frequently miscalculated.
✕
3-hour minimum for every casual and part-time shift Minimum 3 consecutive hours per engagement for both casuals and part-time employees. If someone is sent home early, the 3-hour minimum still applies. Per engagement, not per week.
!
Part-time hours outside written agreement = overtime If a part-time employee works beyond their agreed written pattern with no written record of a variation, those hours must be paid at overtime rates. Text messages recording the agreed variation are sufficient — but they must be kept.
!
Annual rate update — 1 July every year Rates increase each July. Missing the update means every employee is underpaid from day one of the new financial year. Set a payroll calendar reminder for late June — don't assume your provider catches it automatically.
!
Casual conversion — employee choice pathway (2024 law change) Eligible casuals (6 months regular work; 12 months for small businesses) can request conversion to permanent in writing. You must respond in writing within 21 days. Have a documented process in place — this is a Fair Work audit trigger.
✕
Missed breaks create overtime liability If a shift exceeds 5 hours and no meal break is provided, overtime rates apply from when the break should have been taken. This is a pay obligation, not just a conditions breach. Busy weekend rushes are the most common trigger.
!
10-hour break after overtime is mandatory An employee who works overtime must have 10 consecutive hours off before their next shift. If they return early, double time applies until they've had the full 10 hours.
!
Annual leave loading — 17.5% or weekend penalty, whichever is higher Staff who regularly work Saturdays or Sundays are often incorrectly paid just 17.5% loading on annual leave. If the weekend penalty they would have earned is higher, that rate applies.
✓
Classification must reflect actual duties — not job title You cannot keep someone on L1 rates if they routinely do L2 work. Classification is based on the skills and duties required by the employer, effective from when they started doing the work.
✓
Records must be kept for 7 years Classification, pay rates, start/finish times, overtime, allowances, and leave records. Fair Work requests these first in an audit. 7 years — not 5.
✓
Right to disconnect — employers must not prevent it Active from August 2024 (August 2025 for small businesses). Employees can disengage from after-hours work contact. Review how your location managers communicate roster changes and work requests outside of shifts.

⚠ AFL Grand Final Friday

Date announced by the AFL annually — typically late September or early October. It is a Victorian public holiday. Add it to your payroll calendar as soon as it's announced each year, before September rosters are published.

⚠ Melbourne Cup Day

Applies within the Melbourne metropolitan area only. Regional Victoria outlets do not observe this as a public holiday — ordinary rates apply. Confirm the applicable scope for each outlet individually.

Substitute public holidays

When Christmas Day or Boxing Day falls on a weekend, a substitute weekday is observed. If an employee works both days, the public holiday rate applies to only one — the employee chooses which.

Reasonable request to work

You may request an employee work on a public holiday if the request is reasonable. Employees may refuse on reasonable grounds. Document all requests and responses in writing.

Confirm dates each year at business.vic.gov.au. The AFL Grand Final Friday date must be added to payroll calendars manually once announced.
